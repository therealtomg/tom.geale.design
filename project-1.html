QUT Media Campaign

A multi-channel visual marketing campaign to address the lack of knowledge about reporting sexual harm.

Project Presentation

Project Overview

The client (QUT) wanted to address the worrying issue of 58% of students having little or no knowledge of where to seek existing support services or how to report an incident. To combat this, a multi-channel media campaign aiming to increase awareness of reporting options was created.

To suit the needs of this brief, I created a prototype for a campaign mini-site (both mobile and desktop mockups), an Instagram ad and two posters, both portrait and landscape.

Design Process

Research

I started the design process by understanding the brief and researching similar campaigns. I found that most students had no idea where or how to report sexual assault. Based on this, I knew I wanted my campaign to feature easy to digest, straight forward information which clearly explains how to report sexual harm. While being direct, I wanted the messaging and feel of the campaign to be supportive and friendly in tone.

I also undertook a "think, feel, do" exercise to help me better understand how I want the audience to react to the campaign.

Reach out, Speak out, Help out and Experimentation

I begun experimenting with the concept of "Reach out, Speak out, Help out" after re-reading the three key priorities from the brief: be supportive, penetrative and informative.

I played with the idea of stickers and guerrilla marketing campaigns given the catchy title of the campaign. Ultimately I decided a microsite would be the most practical and accessible way of presenting the information to the target audience.

Final Designs

After experimentation and lots of iterative design, I delivered a prototype for a campaign microsite (both mobile and desktop mockups), an Instagram ad and two posters, both portrait and landscape.
